Material Review

  • Peer group websites
  • Kickoff meeting and feedback with key stakeholders

Maria

Maria is the Associate Development Director at a small liberal arts college in the Midwest. Her portfolio is foundation relations and stewardship of grants and donors that support programs at the college. She is on the lookout for new opportunities as well as opportunities with existing foundations.

Maria’s college participates in the Schuler Scholar Program, but as she has been in her position for only three years, she’s not entirely familiar with the program or the foundation. She remembers reading about a new initiative about a year ago, and wonders if the foundation may have opportunities for her college.

Maria

What Maria tells us:

  • She needs a primer on the foundation and what it offers so she can get up to speed.
  • She wants a way to get regular updates from the foundation to know if there are new opportunities.
  • If there are opportunities for her college, she will want to learn the next steps to taking advantage of those.

John

John is an Associate Dean of Students and Professor of Mathematics at an East Coast liberal arts college. He is particularly interested in diversity at the college and in building community among students from different backgrounds.

He has known quite a few Schuler Scholars over the course of 12 years at the college and has played a significant role as a point person for those students and to help create a supportive environment.

John would like to find more ways to support students from diverse backgrounds so that he can forward opportunities to his colleagues in admissions and development.

John

What John tells us:

  • Research on first-generation college student success would be helpful in building support programming.
  • As a motivated connector, he could be a potential conduit to other departments (advancement, college administration) with potential opportunities.
  • He is a potential “cheerleader”: for initiatives, someone who might go on record for future initiatives.

Jeanne

Jeanne is the President of a small liberal arts college — a graduate of Smith College (B.A. History) and Harvard University (Ph.D. Economics) — who began her tenure at the beginning of the COVID-19 pandemic. Until now most of her transition has been dealing with the college’s response to the pandemic and assuring the continuation of the college’s wide base of support.

As life begins to normalize, her focus is now on the college’s future. She read about Jack Schuler in the Chronicle of Higher Education and would like to know more about his foundation.

Jeanne

What John tells us:

  • She needs top-line information about the foundation, how it is funded and where it spends.
  • Search ranking may be a factor in perception.
  • Most of her in-depth research will be done by development staff and likely off site in an effort to understand the family.

Bill

Bill is a writing contributor to Inside Higher Ed, and frequently writes stories and commentary on diversity in higher education. He’s currently writing a story about College Access programs and the success rate of students who participate in these programs. He’d like to understand the foundation’s perspective and research on the long-term success of its participants. As an alum of an HBCU, Bill’s experience was different from many Schuler Scholars and he is interested in that comparison.

His article deadline is Tuesday.

Bill

What Bill tells us:

  • He needs quick access to the information put out by the Foundation and the right people to contact to follow up immediately.
  • Referrals to official streams of communication (Twitter, LinkedIn) will help give him context.
  • He may not be an advocate, so the sooner he gets to a real person, the better.

Ron

Ron is the Director of a family foundation and the Board is interested in College Access programs. Ron has been given a directive to understand the landscape of available programs and to see where the foundation can best serve the field.

The foundation supports programs in several different areas of interest to the family and has been a funding partner with other foundations.

Ron

What Ron tells us:

  • He may need some background in College Access as a whole and where Schuler fits in the grantmaking landscape.
  • Listings of current and past initiatives will help him evaluate if there are good partnership opportunities.
  • He’ll want to connect with his counterpart at the foundation.

Learnings from Stakeholders

To Emulate

  • Information architecture and menu structure that addresses the different types of content seekers
  • Clear and emphatic use of CTA
  • Clear and simple mission statement and transparent and simple communication of the grant-making process
  • Well-curated press section
  • Use of white space and bold, colorful photos

To Stand Out

  • Clean and consistent design (overall on the Schuler Foundation sites)
  • Put the key message in focus on the homepage and support it with curated content
  • Respect accessibility guidelines
  • Clear navigation path for the major personas

Project Plan

Entity Types

  • Initiatives: content type to hold information on current and past initiatives
  • Press Release content type to press releases from the communication department.
  • Resource: content type to hold information research reports
  • Foundation Staff Member: content type to hold information related to Schuler staff members
  • Custom Page w/ layout: a content type for “evergreen” content, which can adapt itself to different layouts to serve the purpose of the particular page. Combined with the use of a reusable block system, this content will serve to make the site “on brand,” but also highly editable for a site admin.
